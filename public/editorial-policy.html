Editorial Policy

Our commitment to accuracy, independence, and evidence-based medical journalism.

Our Mission

MedPulse exists to make peer-reviewed medical research accessible, readable, and actionable. We surface content from the NIH PubMed database — the world's most comprehensive biomedical literature repository — and present it without editorialisation, sensationalism, or commercial bias.

Content Sources

All articles on MedPulse come from one of two sources:

  • PubMed / NCBI: Articles are sourced directly from the National Institutes of Health Entrez E-utilities API. These are peer-reviewed publications indexed in MEDLINE and PubMed Central. Titles, abstracts, author lists, and journal names are reproduced verbatim from the original metadata.
  • Original authored content: Articles written by registered MedPulse authors are reviewed before publication. Authors are responsible for the accuracy and integrity of their submissions.

What We Do and Do Not Do

  • We do not alter, rewrite, or editorially summarise PubMed abstracts.
  • We do not accept paid placement, sponsored articles, or advertiser influence of any kind.
  • We do not provide medical advice. Nothing on MedPulse should be interpreted as clinical guidance.
  • We do not guarantee that any article represents current medical consensus or supersedes professional clinical judgement.
  • We do categorise articles automatically using keyword-based specialty mapping. Miscategorisation may occasionally occur.
  • We do link back to the original PubMed record for every imported article.

Corrections Policy

If a factual error is identified in original authored content, we will correct it promptly and note the correction at the bottom of the article. Errors in PubMed metadata are inherited from NCBI and should be reported to the original journal. We do not correct or alter PubMed-sourced content — it is displayed as-is from the source.

Independence

MedPulse is an independent platform with no pharmaceutical, hospital, insurer, or government affiliation. Editorial decisions about which content to surface are made algorithmically based on PubMed search relevance and recency.

Medical Disclaimer

The content on MedPulse is for informational and educational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of a qualified healthcare provider with any questions regarding a medical condition. Never disregard professional medical advice or delay in seeking it because of something you have read on this platform.
